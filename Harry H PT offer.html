The Arrangement

How It Works

You operate as a self-employed personal trainer based at GymGeek. You run your own business, keep your own clients, and set your own hours outside of a small weekly cover commitment. There is no fixed rent and no upfront cost. You pay only per client, and the referral scheme means the gym can end up paying you.

Why we've set it up this way. Ultimately this is a business, but Joe and Lloyd have both been self-employed PTs working for someone else, and we know first-hand how frustrating that can be. We would rather make money together with someone than because of them. Everything in this proposal is built on that.

Where Your Money Goes

You Help Shape The Gym

A full gym refurb is taking place within the next 6 months, and we will be adding a number of new pieces of equipment as part of it. Any money you do pay us goes straight back into upgrading the gym and the surrounding space, not into anyone's pocket.

And because you would be contributing to it, your input on what we buy for the gym would be as important as our own. The kit you want to coach with is the kit we will be looking to get.

Residual Income

Sign-Ups Pay Your Rent

Anyone you sign up to any of the GymGeek groups or to open gym earns you £25 per month, per person, for as long as they remain a member. There is one condition: you complete their check-ins. That is it. Keep the check-ins up to date and the money is yours, month after month.

Worked Example: 5 Sign-Ups
2 people signed up to open gym
2 × £25 = £50pcm
3 people signed up to groups
3 × £25 = £75pcm
You earn, every month
£125pcm = £1,500 a year
Put another way: those 5 sign-ups alone cover the facility fee on 2 of your PT clients, with £25 left over. Every two sign-ups cancel out one client's fee, and it keeps paying you whether you are on the gym floor or not.
Your Commitment

Gym Floor Cover

You provide 4-8 hours of gym cover each week. We will agree a fixed weekly slot with you, so your cover is predictable and easy to build your diary around. During this time you may cover some group sessions, and you will provide holiday cover for Joe, Lloyd and Lauren when needed.

Your cover hours are part of your rent. This is not working for free and it is not dead time: it is how you pay for the facility. Instead of handing over more cash each month, part of what you give the gym is your time on the floor, and that time works for you while you are there. You sharpen your craft, coach in front of a busy floor, and get face to face with existing members every single week: the exact people most likely to become your clients or refer their friends and family to you. Paying part of your rent in hours rather than money is exactly what takes the financial risk out of going out on your own. You are never paying for an empty diary.

The time is yours. Any free time during your cover hours can be used to train your own clients, create content, or work on your business. We just need someone on the gym floor. Paid floor hours above your agreed cover may also be available from time to time.
Your Clients Get More

Benefits For Your Clients

Every client you train here gets more than a PT session. These are included for all of your members, and they make your offer stronger than any PT working out of a standard gym.

Anytime Open Gym Membership Included

The gym is open 6am to 9pm and we do not shut during the day. Every one of your 1-2-1 clients gets an anytime open gym membership as part of training with you, so they can come in and train whenever suits them, even when you are not here. That is a huge value incentive for anyone deciding whether to sign up with you: one price, a coach and a gym.

Monthly 15-Minute Injury Check-In With Lloyd

Every one of your members gets a monthly 15-minute injury check-in with Lloyd, BSc Sports Therapy and BASRAT certified. These check-ins are huge for retention: they are often the difference between keeping and losing a client because of an injury. A niggle gets looked at before it becomes a reason to quit, and your income stays protected.
