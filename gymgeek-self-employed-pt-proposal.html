The Arrangement

How It Works

You operate as a self-employed personal trainer based at GymGeek. You run your own business, keep your own clients, and set your own hours outside of a small weekly cover commitment. There is no fixed rent and no upfront cost. You pay only per client, and the referral scheme means the gym can end up paying you.

One thing we would need from the start: for any of our existing offerings, you would follow the GymGeek pricing model. It keeps things fair and consistent for every member and every coach, so nobody is undercutting anyone. Beyond that, how you run your business is up to you.

Why we've set it up this way. Joe and Lloyd have both been the self-employed PT grafting to make someone else money, and we know how much that grinds you down. So we built this the other way round: you keep your clients, you keep what you charge bar a simple per-client fee, and we only do well when you do. We would rather earn alongside you than off the back of you.

Residual Income

Sign-Ups Pay Your Rent

Anyone you sign up earns you a monthly payment for as long as they remain a member: £25pcm per group sign-up and £35pcm per Open Gym Plus sign-up (Open Gym Plus is £35 a month). There is one condition: you complete their check-ins. That is it. Keep the check-ins up to date and the money is yours, month after month.

Put another way: those 5 sign-ups alone more than cover the facility fee on 2 of your PT clients, with £45 left over. The referral money keeps coming in whether you are on the gym floor or not.
Your Commitment

Gym Floor Cover

You provide 6-8 hours of gym cover each week. We will agree a fixed weekly slot with you, so your cover is predictable and easy to build your diary around, and some hours may be banked here and there, so there is flexibility around busier and quieter weeks. During this time you may cover some group sessions, and we may use some of these hours as holiday cover for the existing team when needed.

Your weekly cover is essentially the rent. This is not working for free and it is not dead time: your hours on the floor are how you pay for the facility, in place of a cash rent. That time then works for you while you are there. You sharpen your craft, coach in front of a busy floor, and get face to face with existing members every single week: the exact people most likely to become your clients or refer their friends and family to you. Paying part of your rent in hours rather than money is exactly what takes the financial risk out of going out on your own. You are never paying for an empty diary.

The time is yours. Any free time during your cover hours can be used to train your own clients, create content, or work on your business. If you have nothing on and fancy helping with a bit of cleaning, great. If you want to get your own training in, feel free. For us it is simply about the gym floor being covered.
