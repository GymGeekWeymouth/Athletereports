What We Measured
GYMGEEKMetric Glossary
What the numbers mean
A plain-language guide to every metric in this report
CMJ Height
How high off the ground the athlete jumped. A direct measure of explosive leg power.
RSI Modified
Jump height divided by total movement time. Rewards both height and speed of execution — the higher the score, the more explosive and efficient the movement.
Peak Power (rel.)
The highest power output during the jump, divided by bodyweight. Shows how explosively force is generated per kilogram.
Limb Asymmetry (CMJ)
The percentage difference in force between left and right leg during the jump. Under 10% is considered well-balanced; higher values indicate one leg is doing more work than the other.
IMTP Peak Force
The maximum force recorded during a maximal isometric pull against a fixed bar, relative to bodyweight. A measure of overall lower body strength.
CMJ Time to Take-off
The total time from the start of the downward dip to the moment the feet leave the ground, in milliseconds. Lower values indicate a more explosive, efficient athlete.
Sway Velocity
The average speed of the body's centre of pressure while standing still, in mm/s. Lower values indicate better postural stability and balance control.
