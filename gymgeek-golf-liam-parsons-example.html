What We Measured
GYMGEEKAnalyseGolf Metric Glossary
What the numbers mean
A plain-language guide to every metric in this report and its relevance to golf performance
CMJ Height
How high the athlete jumps from a countermovement. Directly correlates with clubhead speed (r≈0.61, Read et al.) — taller jumps reflect greater lower body explosive power applied through the kinetic chain in the downswing.
RSI Modified
Jump height divided by movement time — captures reactive efficiency and how quickly force is expressed. Higher RSI in elite golfers reflects the tendon stiffness and stretch-shortening cycle quality that underpins weight transfer speed through impact.
CMJ Positive Impulse
The total force applied over time during the push-off phase (N·s). Strongly correlated with CHS (r≈0.79, Wells et al. 2019) — the single strongest jump-derived predictor of driving distance in elite golfers.
CMJ Peak Power (rel.)
Maximum power output per kg of bodyweight during the jump. Relates to the ability to generate explosive torque in the lower body — a key driver of swing arc velocity and carry distance.
Squat Jump Height
Jump height from a static start with no countermovement. Isolates pure concentric power — the ability to generate force without elastic rebound. Differences between SJ and CMJ reveal stretch-shortening cycle efficiency.
Drop Jump RSI
Reactive strength from a drop landing — measures fast SSC and tendon elasticity. In golf, this maps onto the ability to rapidly load and unload the trail leg during the backswing-to-downswing transition.
IMTP Peak Force (×BW)
Maximum isometric pull force relative to bodyweight. Reflects maximal lower body strength — significantly related to CHS in elite golfers (r≈0.48–0.75, Wells et al.; Read et al.). Forms the strength foundation from which power is expressed.
IMTP RFD @ 200ms
How fast force is generated in the first 200ms of the IMTP pull. The golf downswing occurs in ~200–250ms — so this directly captures the neuromuscular speed relevant to swing acceleration. Players with higher RFD generate greater ball speed (g=1.13, Wells et al. 2022).
F-V Profile — F0 / V0 / Pmax
The force-velocity (F-V) profile describes the mechanical relationship between a golfer's ability to produce high force (F0) and high velocity (V0). Pmax is where these intersect at peak power. Elite golfers are typically force-oriented (high F0 relative to V0) — matching the demand of accelerating a relatively light club rapidly. The F-V slope tells the practitioner whether to prioritise strength or speed training.
Limb Asymmetry (CMJ)
Left-to-right force difference during the CMJ. Moderate asymmetries are common and not directly detrimental to CHS in golfers (Read et al. 2020) — but large asymmetries may signal load imbalances relevant to injury risk and swing consistency.
Sway Velocity (CoP)
Centre-of-pressure movement speed during quiet standing. Balance control is fundamental to consistent address position, weight shift, and impact repeatability. Elite golfers demonstrate markedly lower sway values than recreational players.
