What We Measured
GYMGEEKAnalyseMetric Glossary
What the numbers mean
A plain-language guide to every metric in this report
Jump Height
CMJ Height. How high off the ground you jumped. A direct measure of explosive leg power.
Reactive Strength
RSI Modified. Jump height divided by total movement time (eccentric + concentric + flight). Rewards both height and speed of execution. GymGeek uses the ForceDecks total-contraction-time method.
Explosive Power
Peak Power (rel.). The highest power output during the jump, divided by bodyweight. Shows how explosively force is generated per kilogram.
Leg Asymmetry
Limb Asymmetry (CMJ). The percentage difference in force between left and right leg during the jump. Under 10% is considered well-balanced.
Maximum Force
IMTP Peak Force. The maximum force recorded during a maximal isometric pull against a fixed bar, relative to bodyweight. A measure of overall lower body strength.
Rate of Force Development
RFD @ 200ms (IMTP). How quickly maximal force is expressed in the first 200 milliseconds of the isometric pull, in Newtons per second. Higher values indicate a faster, more explosive neuromuscular system. Values are sensitive to sampling frequency and onset method; GymGeek norms are calibrated to this protocol.
Postural Sway
Sway Velocity. The average speed of the body's centre of pressure whilst standing still, in mm/s. Lower values indicate better postural stability and balance control.
